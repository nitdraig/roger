What is Sharpe Ratio?

The Sharpe Ratio is a risk-adjusted performance measure, which calculates the additional profitability obtained per unit of risk. It is defined as the difference between the performance of a investment and the risk-free rate, divided by the deviation profitability standard. A higher Sharpe Ratio indicates a better risk-adjusted performance.
